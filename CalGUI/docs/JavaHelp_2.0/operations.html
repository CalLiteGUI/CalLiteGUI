3.6. Operations Dashboard

The operations dashboard has options relating to CVP and SWP system operations, as follows:

-CVP Operations and Facilities.  The checkboxes here can be used to turn on two different wheeling operations (CVC Wheeling and Joint Point of Diversion) and also the Delta-Mendota Canal(DMC)/California Aqueduct Intertie.  For more description of wheeling operations, see the Reference Manual.  The Intertie project allows the DMC and California Aqueduct to share conveyance capacity, thus increasing export capacity.

-CVP/SWP Allocation Methods.  Here the user can choose between different CVP and SWP allocation methods.  Currently the only available allocation method is Water Supply Index/Delivery Index (WSI/DI) Curves, but other methods will be available in future versions of CalLite. Default WSI/DI curves for both CVP and SWP can altered here by clicking on the appropriate box and editing the values in table on the right side of the screen.  The user should be aware that the WSI-DI curves should be edited with caution.  They are carefully developed through iterative running of CalSim II with a particular set of water supplies and demands.  More aggressive allocations may result in reservoir storage conditions that are not able to meet regulations through dry years.  Relaxed allocations may result in storage levels that create higher flows in some months and unexpected modifications to Delta operations that are predicated on antecedent conditions.  Results will not always be what the user intended.  Careful analysis of output is always necessary.  For more information about CalLite allocation methods, see Appendix E of the CalLite Reference Manual.
