3.5.1. D-1641 Regulations Tab

The regulations on this tab are all from the State Water Resources Control Board Decision 1641 (D-1641).  These regulations are summarized below, but for more detail on the D-1641 standards, see Appendix B of the CalLite Reference Manual. Clicking the checkbox next to a regulation will activate it for the current CalLite scenario.  For many of these standards, the specific D-1641 standard can be replaced with a user-defined standard.  If this is desired, after turning on the regulation, click on the radio button for User defined and enter desired values in the editable table (on the right half of the screen).  The month numbers in the user-defined tables refer to USGS water year months, so Oct = 1, Nov =2 etc.     

The regulations on this tab are as follows:

-Delta Cross Channel (DCC).  This defines the numbers of days that the DCC gates are open in each month.  Operation of the DCC assists in transferring fresh water from the Sacramento River south across the Delta.  Operations depend on water quality, flood protection, and fish and wildlife requirements.  The number of days open each month can adjusted using the user-defined option.

-Sacramento River at Rio Vista Minimum Flow.  This defines minimum flows at Rio Vista, which vary by month and water year type.  This standard can be user-defined.

-Minimum Net Delta Outflow.  This defines minimum outflows through the Delta.  The flow standard varies depending on month, water year type, and other criteria as specified in D-1641.  User-defined values can be set by month and water year type.  

-X2 Requirements.  The X2 position is the distance (in kilometers) east of the Golden Gate Bridge of the salinity level of 2 parts per thousand chloride.  The goal of this standard is to maintain the X2 position at or to the west of certain specified locations.  In D-1641 this standard is in place Feb-June and is enforced in different locations (Roe Island, Chipps Island, Collinsville) for different parts of each month depending on certain criteria discussed in Appendix B of the User's Reference Manual.   When user-defined the X2 standard can be turned on or off for each month, values can be set in kilometers by month and water year type, and there is no Roe Trigger. When this standard is user defined, the D1641 X2 on this tab and Fall X2 standard on the Biological Opinion RPAs tab are automatically de-activated. 

-Roe Trigger.  This trigger is normally a part of D-1641 regulations.  Under D-1641 standards, X2 is required to be at or west of Roe Island for a certain number of days if the preceding month's X2 position is west of Roe.  If the preceding month's X2 position was east of Roe, then the required number days west of Roe is automatically set to 0.  If the trigger is not used, then the required number days west of Roe is always 0.

-Export-Inflow Ratio.  This limits the combined exports of CVP and SWP to a specific proportion of total Delta outflow.  The D-1641 standard varies by month and river flow levels.  When user-defined this proportion (ratio) can be adjusted by month.

-Vernalis (Vernalis D-1641 Criteria).  This limits the combined exports of CVP and SWP to the minimum of two different standards during the Apr 15-May 15 pulse period.  The first standard is from D-1641, where exports are limited to the maximum of 1500 cfs or San Joaquin River flow at Vernalis.  The second standard is the VAMP (Vernalis Adaptive Management Plan) export cap, which varies depending on the VAMP pulse flows released during this same period.  To be consistent these VAMP pulse flows are only included in the San Joaquin River flow into the Delta in CalLite when this export cap is on.  When it is off the pulse flow is turned off as well.     

-Salinity Standards at Emmaton and Jersey Point (for agriculture), Rock Slough (for M&I), and Collinsville (for fish and wildlife).  In D-1641 these standards vary depending on month and water year type.

 WARNING: Any user defined values will be used in place of all D1641 regulations. This applies to X2 and Inflow/Export ratio user defined options.
